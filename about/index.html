Founder story

I built the coach's OS because I needed it first.

I'm Mitch Russo. I sold my last software company for eight figures, then ran the 50-coach division for Tony Robbins and Chet Holmes. ClickCoach is what I built when I realized the tools coaches actually need still didn't exist.

Before

At my busiest, I was losing nearly half a day, every day, to the paperwork around coaching.

I sold my software company and began helping other founders scale theirs and prepare them for sale. That work made me a coach almost by accident. As the results compounded, more people came, and within two years I was buried.

Five clients a day. Twenty-plus minutes of admin per session. Notes, homework, dashboards built by hand in spreadsheets, stats pulled together to prove the work was moving the needle. Twenty-five sessions a week. Nearly half my workday gone to paperwork instead of coaching.

I tried five different applications — the ones every coach knows by name. Some were too generic, designed for therapists or trainers. Some were so feature-bloated they slowed me down more than the spreadsheets did. None tracked the things that mattered: assignments, accountability, measurable progress across multiple goals, and the proof points I needed for testimonials and sales conversations.

After months of bending other people's tools to my workflow, I hit a fork in the road. Hire an assistant — or build the software myself. I went the software route.

The realization

The secret wasn't a new coaching framework. It was that coaching gets exponentially more valuable when every assignment, note, and result is captured, structured, and made visible.

Early on, my results were hit-or-miss, and one day I figured out why: I wasn't tracking the work I was giving my clients. They'd agree to an action item on Tuesday, and by the following Tuesday neither of us could remember exactly what got promised, what got done, or what didn't.

I started writing every assignment down and reviewing it at the start of the next session. The improvement was immediate.

Then the second problem surfaced — clients would forget half of what we'd built together. So I started taking deeper notes. The notes worked, but within a year I had hundreds of pages of unstructured material no client could ever actually use.

The third realization was the biggest: I had no statistics. No before-and-after. No graph that said, "This client moved from a 4 to a 9 on revenue confidence." Without that data, I was selling coaching on feeling alone — and feelings are easy for prospects to discount.

That was the operating principle ClickCoach is built on.

Today

My day runs on rails. Admin time is down to ~5 minutes a session — and most of those are spent reviewing AI-generated summaries.

My clients log into their own mobile-friendly portal that holds every goal, every assignment, every note, every uploaded file, every progress chart. They come to sessions prepared. Homework gets done because the system is asking them, not me. I show up calm. I close sessions on time. The work between sessions, which used to be the weakest link in coaching, is now the strongest.

On the back end, my entire practice lives in one place. Scheduling, billing, programs, courses, broadcasts, assessments, the coach directory — all under one login, one mental model. I can coach more clients without working more hours, charge higher rates because the proof is built into the platform, and walk into any sales conversation with measurable transformation in hand.

More than anything, I stopped carrying my business in my head. That quiet — knowing nothing is slipping through the cracks — is the change that mattered most. That's what ClickCoach exists to give every coach.
