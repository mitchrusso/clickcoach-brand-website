The problem I lived

Half my day was paperwork. The other half was apologizing for the paperwork.

Twenty-five sessions a week. Notes scattered across docs and notebooks. Homework I'd promised on Tuesday that neither of us could remember by Friday. Stats spreadsheets I was building by hand to prove the work was moving the needle, because without that data, I was selling coaching on feeling, and feelings are easy for prospects to discount.

I tried five coaching platforms. The big ones. The ones every coach knows by name.

Some were too generic, built for therapists or personal trainers. Some were so complex that they slowed me down more than the spreadsheets did. None of them tracked what actually mattered: assignments, accountability, measurable progress, and the proof I needed for sales conversations.

After months of bending other people's tools to my workflow, I hit a fork in the road. Hire an assistant, or build the software myself. I went the software route.

What I learned — and built in

The Foundation on which ClickCoach is built.

  1. 1

    The work between sessions is the coaching.

    My results were hit-or-miss until I figured out why: I wasn't tracking what I was giving clients between calls. Once I wrote every assignment down and opened the next session by reviewing it, the results changed overnight. ClickCoach is built so the work between sessions never goes missing again.

  2. 2

    Clients forget. The platform shouldn't.

    Deeper notes solved one problem but created another. Now hundreds of pages of unstructured material that no client could ever actually use. The fix wasn't fewer notes. It was structured notes, attached to the human, surfaced when they're needed. Every client in ClickCoach has their own home. Open it before a session, and the last six months are in front of you.

  3. 3

    Without data, you're selling on feeling.

    The biggest realization: I had no statistics. No before-and-after. No chart that said, "this client moved from a 4 to a 9 on revenue confidence." Without that, every renewal conversation was a sales pitch. With it, the chart did the selling. That's why ClickCoach puts client progress charts beside session notes — not buried in a separate report.

What my day looks like now

Today, my admin time is five minutes per session.

Most of those five minutes are spent reviewing AI-generated summaries before they go out. Homework gets done because the system is asking my clients, not me. They show up to sessions prepared because their portal already has everything we built together. I close on time.

My practice runs on one login. Scheduling, billing, programs, courses, broadcasts, assessments — one mental model. I coach more clients without working more hours. I charge higher rates because the proof is built into the platform.

More than anything, I stopped carrying the business in my head. That quiet knowing that nothing is slipping through the cracks is the change that mattered most.
