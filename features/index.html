A week inside ClickCoach

Most software demos hand you a feature list and ask you to imagine the workflow. We'd rather show you the workflow and let you find the features inside it.

Here's what a normal week looks like for a coach running their practice on ClickCoach.

Monday morning — you open one tab, not nine.

You log in once. The dashboard tells you which clients submitted last week's homework, who logged their stats over the weekend, and who's gone quiet. Three new prospects came in through your contact form — they're already in your pipeline with the source tagged.

You spend twelve minutes triaging. The thing that used to eat your whole Monday morning is done before your second coffee.

Tuesday — your 10am session.

You open the live session workspace one minute before the call. The screen already shows last week's summary, this week's homework status, the client's stats charts trending up and to the right, and the three accountability questions you flagged on Friday. Everything you need is in front of you. You don't open another tab.

The session runs. You type a few lines of notes as the client talks. A breakthrough happens at minute 22 — you mark it, attach it to next week's focus.

When you hang up, you click one button. AI cleans up your shorthand, composes the perfect accountability questions for this client this week, then drafts next week's homework, and emails it from your branded template. The client gets it before they've left their desk.

Wednesday afternoon — the admin disappears.

You send next week's homework to four other clients with one click each. AI suggested the focus for each one based on this week's notes. The new client who signed last Friday got her welcome package automatically — onboarding steps, intake assessment, payment terms, signed agreement, all done while you were sleeping.

An invoice paid itself overnight. The receipt is already attached to that client's record. You did not touch your inbox.

Thursday — your group cohort runs itself.

You broadcast a Loom recap to all twelve clients in your group program. The two new members who joined this week were auto-enrolled in the course module everyone else watched last month — they're caught up without you lifting a finger. The aggregated stats report for the cohort lands in your inbox. You actually read it because it took you eight seconds to open.

Friday — the renewal conversation that sells itself.

Sarah's three-month engagement is up. Five months ago you would have walked into that call hoping she'd say yes.

This time you open her progress page first. Revenue line up 34%. Energy score steady at 8 out of 10. Sales calls booked tripled. The chart is sitting there. You don't have to sell the renewal. The chart did.

She signs for another six months before you finish the slide.

By Friday at 5pm, you'll have run a real week of coaching with one login, one mental model, and a paper trail of every promise made and kept.
