What Is Between-Session Coaching Support for Coaches

Learn how between-session coaching support helps clients retain insights, complete homework, stay accountable, and arrive prepared for stronger coaching sessions.

By Mitch Russo · Updated June 18, 2026

Between-session coaching support is the structured practice of providing clients with intentional guidance, accountability tools, and reflection resources between live coaching sessions to sustain and accelerate progress. Coaches who build this support into their practice see clients who are 2.5 times more likely to complete coaching engagements and report higher satisfaction. Methods range from structured journaling and digital portals to AI companions trained on coach-specific frameworks. This is not an optional add-on. It is the structural difference between coaching that produces lasting change and coaching that produces good conversations.

What is between-session coaching support, and why does it matter?

Between-session coaching support, also called intersession support in professional coaching literature, covers every tool, prompt, check-in, and system a coach deploys outside of live sessions. The goal is simple: keep clients engaged with their goals and insights when the coach is not in the room.

The case for making this a core part of your practice is strong. Between-session engagement transforms coaching from isolated conversations into an ongoing collaboration where clients test insights and deepen learning in real contexts. Without it, clients often arrive at the next session having lost momentum, forgotten key takeaways, and reverted to old patterns.

The forgetting curve is the main culprit. Research shows that clients who do not reflect within 24 hours of a session lose roughly 67% of new insights. That means the majority of what you worked through together disappears before the week is out. Structured support between sessions directly counters this loss.

What are the main types of between-session coaching support?

Coaches have more options than ever for keeping clients engaged between sessions. The most effective approaches fall into four categories.

Structured journaling and reflection prompts

Journaling is the most accessible and research-backed form of between-session support. Clients who journal 3–5 times per week show measurably better coaching outcomes, and even two entries per week produce significant improvement. The timing matters as much as the frequency. Post-session reflection within 24 hours locks in insights. A mid-week check-in sustains momentum. A pre-session reflection primes the client for deeper live work.

Coaches who assign specific prompts, rather than open-ended “write whatever you feel” instructions, get far better results. Prompts tied directly to session themes give clients a clear starting point and produce richer material for the next live conversation.

Digital portals and coaching software

Shared digital portals let clients self-manage their action items, document reflections, and track progress between sessions. The coach reviews this work asynchronously, which means no extra live time is required. Coaching tools that consolidate journal prompts, goal tracking, assignments, and resource sharing produce higher client accountability and clearer progress visibility for coaches. This approach creates cumulative progress rather than isolated session insights.

AI-powered coaching companions

AI companions trained on a coach’s specific frameworks and terminology give clients 24/7 access to contextual support. A client stuck on a decision at 10 p.m. can get a prompt or reflection question aligned with their coaching work, rather than waiting two weeks for the next session. The key word is “trained.” Generic AI chatbots cause client disengagement. AI built on coach-specific frameworks produces higher engagement and better session preparation.

Manual check-ins vs. asynchronous systems

Manual check-ins, such as a brief text or voice message from the coach, feel personal but do not scale. Asynchronous systems, where clients log progress and coaches review on their own schedule, scale without sacrificing quality. Most experienced coaches use a combination: automated prompts handle routine accountability, and the coach reserves personal outreach for moments that genuinely need a human touch.

How does between-session support improve client outcomes?

The evidence connecting between-session support to better outcomes is direct and consistent. Clients receiving active support are 2.5 times more likely to complete their coaching engagements. Completion rates matter because clients who finish coaching programs are the ones who refer others and renew contracts.

Engagement with between-session tools also grows quickly once clients experience the value. Usage typically starts at 30–40% and climbs to 60–70% within the first month. That growth is not automatic. It depends on proper onboarding, which means explaining why the tools exist and what clients should do with them from day one.

AI-enhanced support produces a specific benefit that coaches often overlook. When clients can get tactical questions answered between sessions, they arrive at live sessions ready for strategic work. AI-enhanced support increases the perceived value of live coaching by freeing session time for the conversations that only a human coach can lead.

Pro Tip: Send clients a single focused reflection prompt within two hours of every session. Ask them to write three sentences: what they learned, what they will do differently, and what feels uncertain. This one habit alone dramatically reduces the forgetting curve effect.

TPC Coaching Academy describes this shift clearly: between-session work is what turns coaching from episodic conversations into a result-oriented partnership where clients act as active agents in their own development. That framing matters for how you design your practice.

What common pitfalls exist around between-session coaching support?

The biggest misconception coaches hold is that adding between-session support means adding hours of work. The opposite is true. Asynchronous digital tools reduce the manual effort of follow-up, and AI companions handle routine client questions without coach involvement.

Three pitfalls consistently undermine between-session support programs:

  • Generic AI tools. A chatbot with no knowledge of your frameworks gives clients generic responses. Clients disengage quickly. Non-contextual AI causes what practitioners call “chatbot fatigue,” where clients stop using the tool entirely within weeks.
  • No onboarding. Dropping a portal link in a welcome email and expecting clients to use it does not work. Coaches who walk clients through the tools during the first session see dramatically higher adoption rates.
  • Replacing the coach. Between-session support is a structural framework, not a substitute for the coach’s role. The coach remains the primary development agent. Support tools exist to extend the coach’s influence, not replace it.

Pro Tip: Dedicate the last ten minutes of your first session to walking clients through every between-session tool you use. Show them exactly where to log reflections, how to submit questions, and what to expect from any AI companion. Clients who understand the system from the start use it consistently.

How can coaches implement effective between-session support?

Implementation works best when it follows a clear sequence rather than a collection of ad hoc decisions.

  1. Introduce tools during onboarding. Choose your platform before you sign your first client. Whether you use a dedicated coaching platform or a combination of tools, clients need to learn one system, not three.
  2. Educate clients on the “why.” Explain the forgetting curve. Tell clients that journaling within 24 hours of a session protects the insights they just worked hard to reach. Clients who understand the reasoning use the tools more consistently.
  3. Set clear expectations. Define what between-session engagement looks like: how often clients should journal, when they should submit reflections, and how quickly you will review their work. Ambiguity kills follow-through.
  4. Assign specific prompts, not open invitations. Give clients three to five targeted questions after each session. Tie the prompts directly to what you discussed. Vague instructions produce vague reflections.
  5. Use your platform to track and review asynchronously. Review client journal entries and action item updates before each session. This preparation makes live sessions sharper and more focused.
  6. Integrate AI support thoughtfully. If you use an AI companion, train it on your language, your frameworks, and your client’s specific goals. A well-configured AI tool answers the questions clients have at 11 p.m. without pulling you away from your evening.
  7. Balance live and between-session work intentionally. Reserve live sessions for the work that requires real-time dialogue: exploring resistance, making decisions, and deepening self-awareness. Let between-session tools handle accountability, reflection, and tactical questions.

Why I think most coaches are leaving their best work on the table

I have watched coaches build genuinely excellent live sessions and then send clients off with nothing but a vague “think about what we discussed.” That gap between sessions is where most coaching value either compounds or evaporates. The research confirms what I have seen in practice: without structured support, clients lose the majority of their session insights before the week ends.

The shift I advocate for is not about adding more work. It is about designing your coaching practice the way a good curriculum is designed. A teacher does not just lecture and hope students remember. They assign readings, set reflection tasks, and check understanding before the next class. Coaches who think this way build practices where clients arrive prepared, progress faster, and stay engaged longer.

FAQ

What is between-session coaching support?

Between-session coaching support is the structured practice of providing clients with tools, prompts, and accountability systems between live coaching sessions. It includes journaling, digital portals, AI companions, and asynchronous check-ins designed to sustain client progress.

How often should clients journal between coaching sessions?

Clients who journal 3–5 times per week show the strongest outcomes, and even two entries per week produce measurable improvement. The most critical window is within 24 hours of a session, when the forgetting curve is steepest.

Does between-session support increase the coach’s workload?

Asynchronous tools and AI companions handle the majority of between-session client needs without requiring real-time coach involvement. Digital portals let coaches review client progress on their own schedule, which reduces rather than increases manual effort.

What makes AI coaching support effective between sessions?

AI support is effective when it is trained on the coach’s specific frameworks and client context. Generic AI tools produce low-value responses that lead to client disengagement, while coach-specific AI companions increase engagement and improve session preparation.

How quickly do clients adopt between-session support tools?

Client engagement with between-session tools typically starts at 30–40% and grows to nearly 70% within the first month. Strong onboarding during the first session is the primary driver of that growth.
